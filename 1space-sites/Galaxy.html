Overview

The Ēnlū system is situated within the plane of a spiral galaxy specifically classified as an SB-bc barred spiral galaxy. This type of galaxy is characterized by a central, bar-shaped core from which two spiral arms extend. The "SB" designation indicates a barred spiral structure, where a straight, elongated region of stars and interstellar material spans across the galaxy’s center, creating a distinct "bar" shape. The "bc" part of the classification reflects the shape and tightness of the spiral arms – with "b" signifying moderately wound arms and "c" indicating a looser, more open configuration. Together, these features form a unique galactic structure: the bar-shaped core serves as a central anchor, while the spiral arms twist outward across the galaxy's plane, creating the recognizable shape seen in barred spiral galaxies. This arrangement provides the backdrop for the Ēnlū system, positioning it in a dynamic and structured region of the cosmos.

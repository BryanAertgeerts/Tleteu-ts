Overview

The Ēnlū System is a gravitationally bound stellar system centered around the star Ēnlū. This system includes all celestial bodies orbiting Ēnlū, including the planet Tleteuāts, which supports life. Together, these bodies form a dynamic and interconnected network, with the star at its core exerting gravitational influence on each component.

Formation

The Ēnlū System began as a solar nebula, a vast cloud of gas and dust. About 3.962 billion years ago, gravity triggered the nebula's collapse, and Ēnlū began forming at the center. While the star ignited through nuclear fusion, the surrounding material coalesced into a thin protoplanetary disc where the planets, dwarf planets, and other bodies took shape. As nuclear fusion intensified in Ēnlū’s core, the resulting stellar wind swept away much of the remaining gas and dust, leaving behind only solid objects that would become planets, dwarf planets, and smaller system members.

Structure

The Ēnlū System is divided into two main regions: the inner region and the outer region, separated by a boundary known as the frost line at approximately 5.13 astronomical units (AU) from the star. This frost line marks the point at which temperatures are low enough for volatile compounds, like water and methane, to condense into ice. The inner region, closer to Ēnlū, tends to be composed of rocky planets, while the outer region contains gas giants and icy bodies, shaped by the cooler conditions beyond the frost line.

Inner Planets:

The inner Ēnlū system is the domain of the terrestrial planets. Four rocky planets orbit in the inner system: Kle (the first planet), Tleteuāts (the second planet), Klenūtsē (the third planet), and Tsenke (the fourth planet). Kle is a small, rocky, lifeless world orbiting close to the star at a distance of 0.09 AU. Tleteuāts orbits beyond Kle as the second planet of the Ēnlū SYSTEM. It orbits within the system’s habitable zone (0.294-0.423 AU), thus making it the only planet in the planetary system capable of harboring life. Klenūtsē, with a very dense atmosphere, follows as the third planet, closely related to Tleteuāts. Tsenke, the last rocky planet, completes the inner system.

Outer Planets:

The outer system, beyond the frost line, is the domain of the large, gaseous worlds. Two minor gaseous and two major gaseous planets orbit in this region: Ūtsatsa, Chinālo, and Shunāta. Ūtsatsa and Chinālo are the smaller gaseous planets in the Ēnlū SYSTEM, while Shunāta is the largest. These worlds are orbited by multiple moons and have tenuous ring systems. Only Ūtsatsa can be seen from the surface of Tleteuāts. Beyond Shunāta lies an icy debris disk extending from 107.36 AU to 130.06 AU. Many small dwarf planets orbit within the debris disk, none of which are notable or have a significant impact on the system.
